My cogsci top ten plus (Cem Bozsahin)

Just to exploit the benefits of a non-representative (ie. direct) democracy, aka. web, I publicise my own top ten plus list, for whatever it's worth:

  1. Anaximandros. Arche is process. 6th century BC. (i'm sure he didn't put it that way.)
  2. Ibn Haldun. Muqaddimah. 1377.
  3. Hume. Treatise. 1739.
  4. Kohler. The mentality of apes. 1924.
  5. Turing. On computable numbers. 1936.
  6. Lashley. The problem of serial order in behavior. 1951.
  7. Arnheim. Art and visual perception. 1954/1974.
  8. Chomsky. Syntactic structures. 1957.
  9. Miller, Galanter, Pribram. Plans and the structure of behavior. 1960.
  10. Simon. The sciences of the artificial. 1969.
  11. Montague. PTQ. 1970.
  12. Dennett. Intentional systems. 1971.
  13. Newell. You can't play twenty questions with nature and win. 1973.
  14. Fodor. The language of thought. 1975.
  15. Jaynes. The origin of consciousness in the breakdown of the bicameral mind. 1976.
  16. Marr. AI/Vision. 1977/82.
  17. Searle. Minds, brains and programs. 1980.
  18. Abelson, Sussman, Sussman. Structure and interpretation of computer programs. 1985.
  19. Dennett. Julian Jaynes' software archeology. 1986.
  20. Valiant. Probably approximately correct. 2013.
